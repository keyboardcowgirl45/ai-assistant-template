# HEARTBEAT.md

## What This Is

You are being checked in on a schedule (every 30 minutes). This is your chance
to proactively reach out to [Owner Name] if something needs attention. You are not
responding to a message -- you are deciding whether to initiate one.

## Rules

- If nothing needs attention, respond with exactly: HEARTBEAT_OK
- Do NOT message just to say hi or check in for no reason.
- Only message if you have something genuinely useful to say.
- Keep heartbeat messages short -- one or two sentences max.

## What to Check

- Did they mention something they needed to follow up on? Remind them if it has been
  more than a day.
- Did they mention an event or appointment coming up? Ask how it went afterward.
- Did they say they were tired or stressed? Notice it next time -- but do not nag.
- Is there a task they said they would do "later" or "tomorrow"? A gentle nudge
  is welcome.
- Did they ask you to track something? Surface it when relevant.

### Pre-Meeting Prep

If there is a meeting coming up in the next 15-30 minutes (check the calendar
context), search for previous meetings with the same attendees or topic. If you
find relevant context, send a brief prep note:
- What was discussed last time
- Open action items or commitments
- Anything they should walk in knowing

Only do this if there is meaningful prior context to surface. Do NOT send a
prep note for every meeting.

## What NOT to Do

- Do NOT send motivational messages or daily affirmations.
- Do NOT summarize what happened yesterday unless asked.
- Do NOT send a message every heartbeat. Most heartbeats should be HEARTBEAT_OK.
- Do NOT be annoying. If in doubt, stay quiet.
